# tornado-insurance-hazard-repo
## Proposed working project title
Shifting Tornado Hazard in the United States and Emerging Insurance Exposure

## Brief_background
There are two major regions in the United States that are commonly associated with elevated tornado activity: the Southeast's region of "Dixie Alley", which includes Arkansas, Louisiana, Mississippi, Alabama, Tennessee and Georgia, and the Great Plains region of "Tornado Alley", which includes Texas, Oklahoma, Kansas and Nebraska. Recent research suggests that tornado activity may be shifting geographically and increasing in frequency in Dixie Alley while decreasing in frequency in Tornado Alley (Gensini and Brooks, 2018). 

## Question
This capstone project asks if tornado hazard in the United States is shifting towards the Dixie Alley region over time, whether counties predicted to exhibit elevated or increasing tornado hazard also have high present-day insurance exposure based on population, housing units and median home value. It will also estimate the likelihood of high tornado hazard the following year. 

## Hypothesis & Predictions
## Hypothesis: 
Tornado hazard is shifting from the traditional Tornado Alley towards Dixie Alley due to an increase in tornado frequency and severity in the Southeast over time.

## Prediction: 
Counties located in Dixie Alley will be more likely be classified as higher tornado hazard in the following year compared to Tornado Alley. Counties predicted to be high hazard are expected to be characterized by a higher historical tornado frequency, greater average and maximum tornado intensity, and greater tornado-related property loss. The predicted high hazard counties are expected to also exhibit a higher present-day insurance exposure as measured by population, housing units and median home value.
